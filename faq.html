FAQ.

Robots, the app, pricing, and where we are today.

What does Rockio actually sell?

Autonomous robots for commercial farms. Each unit does one job — spray, plow, map, scout. They share a field map through Rockio Fleet, the app you use to assign work and check status.

How does a field get into the app?

RK-SCAN (or a mapping pass with another unit) records boundaries, rows, and obstacles. That map is saved to Rockio Fleet. Every robot on your account uses the same map.

Do I need cell service in the field?

No for day-to-day operation. Robots run from the onboard map. The app syncs when you have connectivity.

Where do the 10× and 73% numbers come from?

Early comparisons against conventional spray and full-size equipment on trial acres in California. We will publish methodology as trials expand. Numbers will vary by crop and operation.

Can I buy robots today?

Not off the shelf. We are in field trials with a small set of growers. Contact us if you want to be considered for early access.

Which robots are closest to shipping?

RK-SCAN and RK-SPRAY are in California field trials. RK-PLOW and RK-SCOUT are in development. Planting and harvest units are in research.

Who handles pesticide compliance?

You do. Rockio logs where and when product was applied. Licensing, labels, and record-keeping stay with the operator.

How is field data stored?

Encrypted in transit and at rest. Details are in our privacy policy. Customer agreements govern retention.
