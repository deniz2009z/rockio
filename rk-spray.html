Operating principle

Blanket spraying is a blunt instrument. RK-SPRAY treats every plant as a discrete target — sensing, classifying, and applying in a single pass.

01

What Rockio does

We build the perception, compute, and actuation stack for precision agriculture — from the sensor suite on the machine to the mission console in your office.

  • 01

    See the field

    Dual LiDAR and RGB stereo build a real-time 3D model of every row. The system operates in dust, low light, and GPS-denied environments.

  • 02

    Classify every target

    Onboard edge GPU runs crop, weed, and pest classifiers at 30 Hz. Each plant gets a treatment decision — not a zone average.

  • 03

    Apply with precision

    12 independently controlled nozzles deliver variable-rate application within 12 ms of detection. Chemical goes on the target, nowhere else.

  • 04

    Prove every drop

    Every application event is logged with coordinates, volume, and chemical type. Audit-ready records for EPA and state compliance.

04

System architecture

05

Subsystems

  1. 02

    Perception housing

    Sealed enclosure for dual LiDAR units and RGB cameras. Downward-facing field of view covers full application swath.

  2. 04

    Fluid reservoir

    120 L chemical tank with level sensing and quick-release fittings. Compatible with standard agricultural formulations.

  3. 05

    Nozzle manifold

    12 individually addressable nozzles with solenoid control. Variable-rate output per nozzle based on plant detection.

  4. 08

    Pump assembly

    High-pressure diaphragm pump with inline filtration. Maintains consistent droplet size across operating pressure range.

  5. 10

    Drive module

    End-mounted wheels with independent motor control. Low ground pressure for row-crop and vineyard operation.

  6. 11

    Application field

    Perception-to-actuation loop runs at 30 Hz. Spray commands issued within 12 ms of target classification.
